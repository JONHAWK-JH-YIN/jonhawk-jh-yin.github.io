Labor-Market Legislative Politics within Hong Kong's Pro-Establishment Bloc after the 2021 Electoral Reform

Independent Research Project

Status: Work in Progress

Fields: Legislative Politics; Hong Kong Politics; Political Economy; Labor-Market Governance

Methods: Legislative Text Analysis; Roll-Call Vote Analysis; Agenda-Setting Analysis

Project Overview

This project studies how pro-establishment legislators in Hong Kong engage with labor-market policy issues after the 2021 electoral reform. It focuses on whether legislators within the pro-establishment camp show different patterns of attention, positioning, or voting behavior across issues such as labor importation, minimum wage, outsourcing, platform work, and employment regulation.

The project is still at an early stage. I am currently collecting legislative materials, organizing policy issues, and developing a coding strategy for analyzing labor-related legislative behavior.

Research Question

The project asks whether pro-establishment legislators in Hong Kong differ in their behavior on labor-market policy issues after the 2021 electoral reform.

More specifically, it examines whether variation in legislative behavior may be related to policy area, sectoral interests, functional constituency backgrounds, labor shortages, employer concerns, or local-worker protection claims.

Data and Materials

The project draws on several types of legislative and policy materials:

• Legislative Council voting records
• Motions and bill texts
• Legislator profiles
• Official labor-shortage data
• Policy documents and government reports
• Materials related to labor importation, minimum wage, outsourcing, platform workers, and employment regulation

Research Design

The project first identifies labor-market issues discussed in the Legislative Council and classifies relevant motions, bills, and policy texts. It then compares legislators' behavior across different labor-related issues.

Possible analytical steps include:

• Classifying labor-related legislative materials by policy issue
• Comparing voting behavior and public positions across labor-market topics
• Linking legislative behavior to member backgrounds, functional constituencies, and sectoral connections
• Examining whether labor-market issues reveal patterns of internal differentiation within the pro-establishment camp

Current Status

This project is currently in progress. I am working on data collection, issue classification, and the development of the empirical strategy. A working paper may be developed after the dataset and coding rules are more fully established.

At this stage, there is no public code repository or replication package. Related materials may be added later once the research design, dataset, and analysis workflow become more complete.
